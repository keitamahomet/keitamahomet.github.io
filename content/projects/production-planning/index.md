This project develops a mathematical optimization model to support production planning decisions within a manufacturing environment. The objective of the model is to determine the optimal production quantities for multiple products while respecting operational constraints such as production capacity, resource availability, and demand requirements.

The problem is formulated using mathematical programming techniques commonly used in operations research and supply chain optimization. The model aims to maximize overall profitability or minimize production costs while ensuring that production levels remain feasible given the constraints imposed by the manufacturing system.

Using the R programming language and optimization tools, the production planning problem is translated into a structured mathematical model where the objective function represents the optimization goal and the constraints represent operational limitations such as production capacities, material availability, and demand fulfillment.

The model evaluates different production scenarios and determines the optimal allocation of production resources across products and time periods. By solving this optimization problem, the model identifies the most efficient production plan that balances operational constraints with economic objectives.

This type of optimization approach is widely used in supply chain management, manufacturing planning, and operations management to improve resource utilization, reduce operational costs, and support data-driven decision-making.

Overall, this project demonstrates how prescriptive analytics and optimization techniques can be applied to solve complex production planning problems and improve operational efficiency in manufacturing systems.
